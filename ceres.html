The Dwarf Planet Ceres

Ceres is the largest object in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter, making up nearly 40% of its mass. This dwarf planet was the first asteroid discovered, in 1801, and is named after the Roman goddess of agriculture.

Ceres has a rocky core and an icy mantle, and recent observations suggest the presence of water vapor, hinting at the possibility of subsurface oceans. Its surface is dotted with craters and mysterious bright spots, believed to be deposits of salt left behind by evaporating water.

As a celestial body, Ceres provides scientists with insights into the formation of planets and the early solar system.
