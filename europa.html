Europa

About Europa

Europa, one of Jupiter's largest moons, is covered in a thick layer of ice. Beneath this frozen crust lies a vast ocean, which scientists believe might harbor the conditions necessary for life. It is one of the most promising places in the solar system to search for extraterrestrial life.

Key Facts

  • Diameter: 3,121.6 km (slightly smaller than Earth's moon).
  • Orbital Period: 3.5 Earth days around Jupiter.
  • Surface Temperature: Approximately -160°C (-260°F).
  • Atmosphere: Thin oxygen atmosphere, not breathable for humans.
  • Subsurface Ocean: Estimated to contain more than twice the water of all Earth's oceans combined.
  • Surface Features: Crisscrossing cracks and ridges made of ice, with few impact craters.

Interesting Insights

The surface of Europa is one of the smoothest in the solar system, leading scientists to theorize that liquid water or slush periodically resurfaces it, erasing older impact craters. The presence of a global subsurface ocean makes Europa a strong candidate for future exploratory missions, including the NASA Europa Clipper mission.
