The Dwarf Planet Pluto

Pluto, once considered the ninth planet of our solar system, is now classified as a dwarf planet. It lies in the Kuiper Belt, a region of icy bodies beyond Neptune. Discovered in 1930, Pluto has a unique orbit and features a thin atmosphere that expands and collapses as it moves closer to and farther from the Sun.

Pluto's surface is a mix of frozen nitrogen, methane, and carbon monoxide. Its largest moon, Charon, is almost half its size, forming a unique binary system. Despite its reclassification, Pluto continues to capture the fascination of scientists and the public alike.
