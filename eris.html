Eris: The Distant Ice Giant

Eris, named after the Greek goddess of discord, is the most massive known dwarf planet, even more massive than Pluto. It resides in the scattered disc region of the solar system and has one known moon, Dysnomia.

Eris's surface is covered with a layer of frozen methane, giving it a bright, reflective appearance. Its discovery in 2005 led to the reclassification of Pluto as a dwarf planet.

Eris’s distance from the Sun makes it one of the coldest known objects in the solar system, with surface temperatures dropping below -240°C (-400°F).
