About Proteus

Proteus is the second-largest moon of Neptune and one of its darker and more irregularly shaped satellites. It was discovered in 1989 by the Voyager 2 spacecraft, making it one of the last major moons to be found in the solar system.

With a diameter of about 420 kilometers (260 miles), Proteus is one of the largest bodies in the solar system that is not spherical in shape. Its surface is heavily cratered and extremely dark, reflecting only about 6% of sunlight, which makes it one of the darkest objects in the solar system.

Proteus orbits Neptune at a distance of approximately 117,600 kilometers (73,100 miles). It has a nearly circular orbit and completes one revolution around Neptune every 26 hours.
