Earth's Moon

About Luna

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite. It is the fifth-largest satellite in the Solar System and the largest relative to its planet. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing Earth's climate and creating ocean tides.

Key Facts

  • Diameter: 3,474 km (about 1/4th the size of Earth).
  • Distance from Earth: 384,400 km.
  • Orbital Period: 27.3 days to complete one orbit around Earth.
  • Surface Features: Rocky, dusty surface with large craters and vast plains.
  • Gravity: 1/6th of Earth's gravity.
  • Atmosphere: No significant atmosphere, meaning no protection from meteoroids and radiation.

Interesting Insights

The Moon is the closest celestial body to Earth, and it has been a focal point for exploration. It is the only place humans have visited beyond Earth. The Apollo missions brought back a wealth of knowledge, and recent advancements are focusing on returning humans to the Moon under NASA's Artemis program.
